What is Dental Care?

Dental care refers to the practice of maintaining the health and hygiene of your teeth, gums, and overall mouth. It includes both preventive measures like daily brushing, flossing, and regular dental check-ups and treatments for existing issues, such as cavities, gum disease, or missing teeth.

FAQ's

A dental crown is a custom-made cap placed over a damaged or decayed tooth to restore its shape, strength, and appearance. Dentists often recommend crowns for teeth that are cracked, worn down, or have undergone root canal treatment. Crowns can also be used to cover dental implants or support dental bridges.

A dental bridge replaces one or more missing teeth by anchoring artificial teeth (pontics) to adjacent natural teeth or implants. The bridge fills the gap left by missing teeth, restoring function and aesthetics. Proper oral hygiene and regular dental check-ups are essential to maintain the longevity of a dental bridge.

It's generally recommended to visit the dentist every six months for routine check-ups and cleanings. Regular visits help in early detection of potential issues, such as cavities or gum disease, and ensure that your oral health is maintained effectively.
